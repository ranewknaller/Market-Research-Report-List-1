<p><ul><li>Thermoplastic Resin</li><li>Thermosetting Resin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic resin market is primarily divided into two types: thermoplastic and thermosetting resins. Thermoplastic resins can be repeatedly melted and reshaped, making them versatile and ideal for applications such as packaging, automotive components, and consumer goods. In contrast, thermosetting resins, once cured, cannot be remelted or reshaped, providing excellent durability and heat resistance, making them suitable for applications in electrical insulation, adhesives, and coatings. Both types cater to diverse industries, driven by specific performance requirements and manufacturing processes.</p></p>
