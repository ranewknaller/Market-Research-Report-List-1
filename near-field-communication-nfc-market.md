<p><p>Near-field communication (NFC) is a short-range wireless technology facilitating data exchange between devices within close proximity, typically 4 centimeters or less. The NFC market can be segmented into two types: non-auxiliary products and auxiliary products. Non-auxiliary products include smartphones and payment terminals that directly utilize NFC for transactions and data transfer. In contrast, auxiliary products encompass supporting technology, such as NFC tags and readers, that enhance functionality and user experience in applications like smart advertising and access control.</p></p>
