<p><p>Ionization chambers are devices used to measure ionizing radiation, categorized into three primary types: air, liquid, and solid ionization chambers. Air ionization chambers utilize gases, typically air, to detect radiation, making them widely used in medical and environmental applications. Liquid ionization chambers use a liquid medium, providing greater sensitivity for specific isotopes. Solid ionization chambers exploit solid materials as the ionizing medium, offering enhanced stability and durability, making them suitable for various industrial applications. Each type caters to specific measurement needs and environments.</p></p>
