<p><p>Offline regulators are devices that manage output voltage and current independent of the power supply's instantaneous voltage. In the current mode market, regulation is achieved by controlling the output current, ensuring a constant current flow which is crucial in applications like battery chargers. Conversely, the voltage mode market focuses on maintaining a stable output voltage, adjusting based on load variations. Both types are essential in various electronic applications, providing reliability and efficiency in power management systems.</p></p>
