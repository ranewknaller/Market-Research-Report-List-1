<p><p>The coco peat market is divided into three main types: brown fiber, white fiber, and others. Brown fiber, derived from mature coconut husks, is commonly used in horticulture for soil conditioning due to its high moisture retention. White fiber, obtained from younger coconuts, is finer and often used in products like handicrafts and mats. The 'others' category includes various applications such as animal bedding, erosion control, and bioengineering. Together, these types cater to diverse agricultural and industrial needs.</p></p>
