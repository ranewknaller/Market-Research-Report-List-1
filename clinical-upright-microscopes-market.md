<p><ul><li>Monocular</li><li>Binocular</li><li>Trinocular</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Clinical upright microscopes are categorized into three main types based on optical design. Monocular microscopes have a single eyepiece, making them compact and suitable for simple tasks. Binocular microscopes feature two eyepieces, allowing for enhanced depth perception and comfort during prolonged use, ideal for detailed observations. Trinocular microscopes include an additional third tube for a camera attachment, enabling simultaneous viewing and imaging, which is beneficial for documentation and sharing findings in clinical and research settings.</p></p>
